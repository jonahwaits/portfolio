Rob Demovsky: Human Interest Story

 

By: Jonah Waits

 

One might imagine that working for a large media company like ESPN would require a lot of bodies to do one job, but for Rob Demovsky that’s not the case. Demovsky runs a one-man show writing and reporting for ESPN.com and occasionally appearing on their daily shows.

 

“I talk to him [my boss] once a week or so, and kind of give them an idea of, hey, this is what I'm working on. You know, and we kind of go from there.”

 

Demovsky came to ESPN from the Green Bay Press-Gazette, where he worked from 1997 to 2013. Demovsky is an open reporter, willing to tell anyone what he does, how he got where he is, and giving advice to students and individuals who also have a passion for reporting.

 

For Demovsky, his job is perfect, and while he technically has a boss, Demovsky works for himself. Demovsky chooses the stories he wants to write and interviews the players he wants to interview. And while he is on deadlines, he works when he wants to. But to Demovsky, that’s not the best part. He loves the dissimilarity in every day.

 

“That's [the dissimilarity is] the best part about it,” Demovsky said. “It's not it's not nine to five, like you don't you don't show up for work at a certain time and walk out at a certain time.”

 

In 2013, ESPN decided to have a single reporter cover every NFL team, rather than a single reporter cover all teams in a division. Rob knew this was the chance of a lifetime and threw his name in the ring. He had some help from one of his closest colleagues and fellow reporter, Kevin Seifert, who at the time was covering all four teams in the NFC North.

 

“I just thought he was a really hard worker,” Seifert said. “I trusted him felt confident that if I recommended him, that the bosses would be impressed.”

 

Demovsky made it clear though; he has done a wonderful job in his career, but he didn’t do anything particularly special to get the job.

 

“It's a communications business and you have to communicate with people, you know, while you do your job, but it also means just, you know, making contacts with people.”

 

Demovsky is now in a position and comfortability in his career that uniquely qualifies him to give some advice to aspiring sports reporters.

 

“Don't be afraid to talk to people,” Demovsky said. “It's amazing how many people in this business are afraid to communicate with people, like I wouldn't have gotten this job if I didn't reach out and make friends with people in the business, but just make contacts with people in the business and, and even doing stories, you have to communicate, otherwise you're not going to get good information.”
